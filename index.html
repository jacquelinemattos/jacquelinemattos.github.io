About me

I have a BSc in Biological Sciences from São Paulo State University (Brazil), with a year abroad studying at the University of Liverpool (UK). At the moment I am pursuing my PhD degree, having my research focused on genomics and bioinformatics for an evolutionary biology approach. For research purposes, I have had training in both R and Python, and I am also skilled in Linux command line, bash scripting, SQL, High Performance Computing (HPC), GitHub and Git version control, and many genomic/bioinformatics frameworks and tools for analyses of NGS data. I have experience with de-novo genome assembly, chromosome-level assembly, and transcriptomic assembly as well. In addition, I have skills with many programming languages and web/software development tools, such as JavaScript, PHP, HTML, CSS.

Research Interests

I am mainly interested in using highly innovative tools and technologies in the study of biological diversity and evolutionary processes, focusing on the Plantae and Fungi kingdoms. My main passion today is using bioinformatics in the study of genomic and transcriptomic data, but I am also interested in developing new software for the investigation of biological systems. My excitement for science continues to grow everyday, and the possibility of developing new methods and analyses that can help the advancement of biology is what drives me towards working and studying more.
